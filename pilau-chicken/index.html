Pilau

Ingredients

  1. 1/2 kg Basmati rice, washed (for better results I highly recommend Basmati reis)
  2. 1/2 kg potatoes peeled, washed and coarsely chopped
  3. 1/2 kg beef, chicken or fish filet cubed
  4. 1 small cup sunflower oil (or any other liquid oil)
  5. 4 cups of hot water or broth
  6. 1 onion, chopped
  7. 5 cloves of Garlic, crushed
  8. 1 fresh Ginger, crushed
  9. 2 fresh Tomatoes , sliced
  10. 2-3 teaspoon Pilau spices
  11. Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure (1hour)

  • Boil beef or chicken with ginger for 10 minutes. Add potatoes and let them boil for 5 minutes then set aside (separate the cooked ingredients from the broth so you can use it later)
  • Heat oil and fry onions till light brown, add garlic and Pilau spices and on a low heat, fry for 1 minute
  • Add tomatoes meat/chicken with potatoes and cook till tender
  • Add rice and ensure to mix everything very well before adding your broth or hot water then stir the mixture very well
  • Add salt and pepper to taste then cover the pot and cook on medium heat
  • When the Food is nearly dry, lower down the heat to very low, cover your Pilau with aluminium paper (please avoid newspapers or polythene papers) and place the lid on top. Leave to cook for 10 minutes .
  • Here you are, your Pilau is ready!
  • Serve your Pilau hot with Kachumbari and Pilipili ya Maembe
  • A banana can also be used for garnishing
  • Some like to serve it with a tasty tomato sauce or beef/chicken stew on the side which is also very nice.

Chicken

Ingredients

  • 1 full size chicken
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 garlic bulb
  • small piece ginger
  • 3 large tomatoes
  • 1 bunch coriander

Procedure

  1. Boils the chicken until soft (more than 1 hr)
  2. Wash and chop your vegetables. Garlic and ginger blended or thinly sliced. Coriander thinly chopped and tomatoes and onions diced.
  3. First stir fry your onions and ginger and garlic until slightly brown then add the chicken.
  4. Cook the chicken for a while to let it soak up the ginger and garlic flavour.
  5. Finally add the tomatoes and coriander. Cook until the tomatoes form a thick stew.
  6. Serve with the freshly cooked pilau
